Big Red Trade

Timeline: September 2023 - November 2023

Introduction

Working in a small group, we aimed to design a solution for students on Cornellâ€™s campus to facilitate buying and selling items
Our starting goal was simple: to understand the motivations, preferences, challenges, and needs of Cornell students living off-campus regarding buying, selling, and exchanging items.
Next, we planned to use this information in hopes of designing a platform or solution that eases the process, reduces environmental waste and unnecessary expenditures.

User Insights


For our data analysis, we started with an affinity diagram. Since we were a remote team, we chose to use Mural to help us gather insights. Our original diagram was very large, after taking insights from over twelve interviews. We ended up condensing it into five main categories, which later became our key insights.




Persona


We created our persona based off the key insights of our affinity diagram. The goal was to make it someone who was a member of our target audience.



Prototypes


Low Fidelity

We started off by independently creating out own low fidelity prototypes. Mine was constructed from copy paper and vellum paper. I hand-drew every screen and used vellum and dry-erase markers for user inputs. When it came to conducting a usability test, I realized how difficult it would be create an app from scratch without any of the development tools and plugins I was used to. I started by identifying which tasks I needed my prototype to feature, and decided on the primary screens. Then I had to work out the flow and functionality between the primary screens, which meant more screens in between. Once I found a user, I acted as the "computer", changing the screens as they made their selections.


A Couple Issues...

We soon began working on digitalizing screens using Figma for our medium fidelity prototype.
After another round of usability tests, we realized that this prototype was riddled with issues we had completely overlooked as designers.
There were countless violated heuristics, including cosmetic level issues like lack of consistency or lack of system visibility, to catastrophic issues like lack of error recovery in certain areas.

High Fidelity

In our high-fidelity prototype, we made many improvements to the app's design and functionality. Initially, there were numerous issues when filtering and browsing, including cut-off screens at the notch, flawed filter systems, and an unresponsive search bar. These violated the visibility heuristic but thankfully these were very simple fixes.
As for more major issues such as a lack of error record, we incorporated screens that would ask for user confirmation when performing a function. And of course, if they want to undo an action they can with almost every action.
